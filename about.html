About me

Hi, I'm Fejiro.
Nice to meet you.

I'm a Toronto-based product designer who genuinely cares about the people on the other side of a screen. I started in Information Technology at York University (graduated 2022), then followed my curiosity into UX design at Sheridan College (graduated 2024). That path wasn't accidental — I wanted to understand how things are built and how they feel to the people using them.

That combination gives me an edge I genuinely value: I can sit with developers and talk constraints, read code when I need to, and still be the person in the room asking "but what does this feel like for a first-time user?" Fewer handoff surprises. Better outcomes.

My process always starts with people. I've sat in parking lots observing car wash users. I've conducted interviews across four countries. I've tested betting flows with people who'd never placed a bet. The fieldwork is where the real design decisions get made — everything in Figma just follows.

When I'm not designing, you'll find me shooting on film (there's something about the patience and intentionality of analog photography that I love), watching films, listening to music, gaming, or suffering through another Manchester United match. I'm also a lifelong Harry Potter fan — the world-building never gets old.

I'm currently open to freelance projects, collaborations, and full-time roles. If you have a problem worth solving, I'd genuinely love to hear about it.
